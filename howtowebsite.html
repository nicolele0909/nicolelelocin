How to Breathe

Breathing is one of the most essential aspects of human function. By practicing breathing everyday, your life could be improved tremendously. Not only would your mental state be more stable and healthy, but breathing also helps you sleep better at night.

If you're searching for a way to breathe properly and normally, here's the perfect article on your favorite How To site.

How to Inhale

As simple as it sounds, inhaling might be a challenge when your body and head decide that they are too tired for the simple act. If anxiety and nervousness make it easy for your body to function improperly and your mind to be clouded with doubt, your lungs––and your heart––are telling you to breathe in as deeply as possible.

Taking in a sufficient amount of oxygen helps your body and mind more aware and alert. Consequently, this is one of the most basic functions of the human activity that is also included in yawning. According to Dr. Gupta from KidsHealth.org, yawning helps us bring more oxygen into the blood. Inhaling oxygen is an inevitable process in aiding our blood circulation working efficiently.

In addition, oxygen is an essential element in respiration. Without O2, energy from the release of food production consumed would be stored permanently. Lungs take in oxygen for the combustion of food, helping release carbon dioxide from blood.

Take your time, clear your head, and take in the largest amount of oxygen in your life. You can feel the sense of breeze brush against the tips of your nostrils as the oxygen flows into your system––and into your blood. To help the process more manageable, imagine yourself standing in your favorite place, being your bedroom or Niagara Falls, with your eyes shut. Visualizing yourself absorb all of the energy and aroma from the place would certainly help guide your way through if you have problem focusing.

How to Exhale

Exhaling is easy just like that; however, according to Alex Russo in Wizards of Waverly Place: "Everything is not what it seems." When in doubt, just remember that taking out CO2 is as important as taking in O2. As you breathe out a certain amount of carbon dioxide, the respiratory cycle contributes tremendously to the adjustment of the pH level in your body. Unlike aerobic organisms such as octopus, mammals, including human beings, need oxygen in and carbon dioxide out of their system.

To calm your breathing down, after you have absorbed all the oxygen possible either through your nostrils or mouth, release the compressed air in your lungs. You can also apply the instruction above from inhaling to your exhaling exercise. As you envision yourself in somewhere comfortable and familiar enough to pacify your mind and body, you can relax into the exhaling.

How to Adjust Your Breathing

Now that you're adept at inhaling and exhaling, as well as comprehending the fundamentals of human biology, it's time to be an expert at it. Knowing when to accelerate or brake your breathing helps your body adjust well to various situations. Such circumstances holding you down can be a test to your circular breathing. At times like this, you can calm your breathing by take counts of your inhaling and exhaling.

Remember when you used to––or still do––count sheep or imagine numbers in your head to sleep better at night? Similarly, counting would help regulate your deep breathing, bringing you into relaxation. According to Anxieties.com, to cope with the excitement inside you, you can count down from ten while closing your eyes and open them again when you reach one. Regulating your breathing by counting is a scientific method that is widely regarded.

How to Avoid Distractions

While breathing, there might be more than one factor affecting your complete concentration. Not only can extraneous factors disrupt your process, but you can yourself unintentionally. Coughing and sneezing might seem harmless enough to ignore, but don't be fooled by their nature as they constrain your body. Edblad from Better Humans confirms that coughing and sneezing or snoring make our breathing lose its rhythm, then force our body to compensate to fill in that void of oxygen.

Breathing silently and rhythmically can hep you adjust your way back to circular breathing. As you make as least noise as possible, your body does not have to waste any energy into conducting activities. Relax your mind and body into the flow of your own world of breathing.

How to Relax Your Mind into Breathing

Are you getting tired of having to breathe all the time? What if I tell you that there certainly is a way to enjoy your time when you inhale and exhale the sense of nature?––because, yes, you can meditate to complete the process. It is true that meditation brings out the best of you physically and mentally. One of the greatest techniques of meditating is called mindfulness meditation, in which you can face your overwhelming thoughts without being scared of the tendency. Regardless of the bad and the good of the overthinking ideas in your head, being conscious and aware of the arising problems can balance your mental institutions.

In addition to conventional meditation, the Internet world offers us a wide variety of ways to pacify your mind after a long hard day. You can download whichever applications that suit you best on your smartphones. Scheduling meditating sessions has never been easier.
